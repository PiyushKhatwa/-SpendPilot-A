# Economics

## Unit Economics Hypothesis

Assumptions for an early MVP:

- 1,000 landing visitors per month.
- 12 percent start an audit.
- 45 percent complete an audit.
- 25 percent of completed audits submit email.
- 8 percent convert to paid report or subscription.

This produces:

- 120 audit starts.
- 54 completed audits.
- 14 captured leads.
- 4 paid conversions.

## Revenue Scenarios

| Offer | Price | Monthly Customers | MRR or Revenue |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| One-time report | $29 | 20 | $580 |
| Team subscription | $99/mo | 10 | $990 MRR |
| Consultant audit pack | $399 | 5 | $1,995 |

## Cost Drivers

- OpenAI summaries are small, bounded requests.
- Firestore usage is low because audits are simple documents.
- Resend email volume is tied to lead capture.
- Hosting cost should remain low until analytics and account history grow.

## Break-Even Thinking

If monthly infrastructure cost is $50 to $100, the product can break even with a small number of one-time reports or one subscription customer. The harder economic problem is distribution, not compute cost.
